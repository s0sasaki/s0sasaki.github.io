Help & frequently asked questions

How does MindGap work?

MindGap intercepts your habit of opening distracting apps by routing each open through a brief countdown. You create a Home Screen Shortcut that points to MindGap's URL for a target app. When you tap that shortcut, MindGap shows how many times you've opened the app in the last 24 hours and makes you wait that many seconds before you can continue.

Setting up a Shortcut

  1. Open MindGap and tap + to add an app.
  2. Tap the app's row — this copies its MindGap URL to your clipboard.
  3. Open the Shortcuts app → tap + → Add Action → search Open URLs → paste the MindGap URL.
  4. Tap the shortcut's name at the top → Add to Home Screen → name it after the real app → tap Add.
  5. Remove the real app's icon from your Home Screen (long-press → Remove from Home Screen). It stays in your App Library.
  6. From now on, use the new icon instead of the original.

The countdown seems wrong / shows 0

The countdown equals the number of times you've opened that app in the last 24 hours. The first open of each day has no wait — that's intentional. Each subsequent open adds one second.

How do I set a session limit?

Tap an app's row on the home screen, then tap Session limit in the menu that appears. Choose a duration (1–60 minutes). MindGap will send you a notification when the time is up, prompting another check-in.

I'm not receiving session-limit notifications

  • Go to Settings → MindGap → Notifications and make sure notifications are enabled.
  • Session-limit notifications are marked Time Sensitive, so they bypass Focus modes — but they still require notifications to be allowed.
  • Make sure you tapped Open [App] in the intervention screen. Notifications are only scheduled after you proceed.

Can I add an app that isn't in the suggestions list?

Yes. Tap +, scroll to the bottom of the suggestions, and tap Add custom app. Enter the app's name and its URL scheme (e.g. spotify://). You can find URL schemes for most apps with a quick web search.

How do I remove a tracked app?

Swipe left on the app's row on the home screen and tap Delete.

Does MindGap collect any data?

No. Everything is stored locally on your device. MindGap has no server, no account, and sends no data anywhere. See the full Privacy Policy.
